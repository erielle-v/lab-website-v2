TISHUMAP

TISHUMAP: Target Inference from Spatialomics & Histology Using Multimodal AI & Phenotypes

Target discovery remains one of the central challenges in drug development, with roughly 90% of drugs entering clinical trials never reaching the market. The leading cause is a lack of efficacy, often traced back to early-stage models that are unable to capture the complexity of human disease and translate poorly to clinical outcomes. This points to a clear need for new methods to identify more promising targets.

Around 70% of existing drugs target cell-cell interactions (most prominently, Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors), indicating that a substantial proportion of exploitable disease biology resides in cell-cell communication. However, it has been difficult to access this space, as conventional sequencing methods require tissue dissociation, which destroys the spatial context needed to determine which cells are interacting, and through which specific molecules in situ. Spatial omics addresses this limitation by enabling the identification of cell-cell interactions in their native tissue context.

TISHUMAP is a spatial-omics-driven target discovery platform built on archived tissue samples from an Asian patient cohort in Singapore, spanning a broad range of cancers and inflammatory diseases. By integrating spatial omics data, digital pathology, and clinical metadata, TISHUMAP creates a "Google Maps" for biomarker discovery, patient stratification, and the identification of targetable cell-cell interactions.

The Platform

  • We are generating molecular-resolution spatial omics data from 2,000 patients with fibrotic and inflammatory conditions.
  • We are curating Whole Slide Images from our large existing SingHealth tissue image repository, part of a national initiative on digitalisation of tissue banks and slide archives.
  • For all patients, we have longitudinal clinical data on diagnosis, treatment, response, and outcome.
  • The platform spans thousands of samples across multiple common cancers (including lung, breast, colon, liver, gastric) and fibrotic conditions (including liver and kidney fibrosis).
  • We apply AI tools to this trimodal dataset to identify targetable cell communication molecules that correlate with patient outcome, and to support patient stratification and mechanism-of-action inference.

We believe TISHUMAP will position Singapore as a hub for target discovery and diagnostics development in Asia.

A multi-institutional collaboration between the Genome Institute of Singapore (GIS, A*STAR), Bioinformatics Institute (BII, A*STAR), National Cancer Centre Singapore (NCCS), and Singapore General Hospital (SGH).
