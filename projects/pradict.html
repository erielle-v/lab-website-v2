PRADICT (Predicting Risk of Age-Related Diseases to Intervene and Change the Trajectory)

By 2030, one in four people in Singapore will be over 65. Many of them will spend a decade or more of that in poor health, not because ageing itself is inevitable in its current form, but because we tend to catch disease only once it's already doing damage. PRADICT is trying to move that timeline earlier.

We're profiling blood samples from Chinese, Malay, and Indian participants in Singapore's National Precision Medicine Programme, using multi-omics: genotyping, capture sequencing to detect clonal haematopoiesis, and single-cell RNA sequencing.

Where this is headed: a resource that goes from biomarker discovery through to validated drug targets, handed off to Singapore's therapeutics development pipeline. Part of that is the usual work of treating age-related disease. Part of it is newer territory: drugs aimed at ageing itself, not just what ageing causes.

We welcome questions, collaborations, and new ideas, so please get in touch with us!
